1. Government & Politics

Nepal is a federal democratic republic located in South Asia. The country officially became a republic in 2008 after abolishing the monarchy.

The President is the ceremonial head of state, while the Prime Minister is the head of government with executive powers.

Nepal has a multi-party political system. Major political parties include Nepali Congress, CPN-UML, and CPN (Maoist Centre).

The Constitution of Nepal was adopted in 2015, establishing seven provinces and a federal structure.

Nepal maintains diplomatic relations with both India and China, balancing its foreign policy between the two neighbors.

2. Himalayan Peaks

Nepal is home to 8 of the world’s 14 highest mountains, including Mount Everest (8,848.86m), the tallest peak on Earth.

Other major peaks include Kanchenjunga, Lhotse, Makalu, and Cho Oyu.

The Himalayas were formed by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates millions of years ago.

These mountains are a major source of tourism, attracting climbers and trekkers from around the world.

Sagarmatha National Park protects the Everest region and its fragile ecosystem.

3. Culture & Religion

Nepal is a multicultural and multi-ethnic country with over 120 ethnic groups and languages.

Hinduism and Buddhism are the two dominant religions, often blending in traditions and festivals.

Major festivals include Dashain, Tihar, Buddha Jayanti, and Holi.

The living goddess tradition of Kumari is unique to Nepal, especially in Kathmandu Valley.

Traditional music, dance, and Newari architecture reflect Nepal’s rich cultural heritage.

4. Trekking Routes

Nepal is one of the world’s best trekking destinations.

Popular trekking routes include Everest Base Camp, Annapurna Circuit, Annapurna Base Camp, and Langtang Valley.

Trekking seasons are mainly spring (March–May) and autumn (September–November) for clear skies.

Trekkers need permits like TIMS card and conservation area permits depending on the region.

Teahouse trekking is common, where travelers stay in local lodges along the route.

5. Economy

Nepal’s economy is primarily based on agriculture, tourism, and remittances from abroad.

Agriculture employs a large portion of the population, with rice, maize, and wheat as main crops.

Tourism, especially trekking and mountaineering, contributes significantly to GDP.

Remittances from Nepali workers abroad form a major source of national income.

Hydropower is an emerging sector with huge potential due to Nepal’s river systems.

6. Wildlife & Parks

Nepal is rich in biodiversity due to its varied geography from plains to mountains.

Major national parks include Chitwan National Park, Sagarmatha National Park, and Bardiya National Park.

Rare animals found in Nepal include the Bengal tiger, one-horned rhinoceros, red panda, and snow leopard.

Chitwan National Park is famous for jungle safaris and rhino conservation.

Conservation efforts are managed by the Department of National Parks and Wildlife Conservation.
